How to read this. The seven categories below are ranked as go-to-market priorities for Reno by combining three things: (1) size — how much money the vertical puts into local advertising, (2) growth — how fast that spend is rising, especially in streaming/CTV where Spectrum Reach competes, and (3) Reno fit — how concentrated the advertiser base is in this specific market. Each card lists the fastest-growing sub-categories (⭐ = the "dive-deeper" candidates, the way gaming was flagged last year). Important data caveat: no public dataset breaks out ad spend by category for a single DMA (your own Heatmap methodology documents this — Borrell/BIA/Kantar are paid and county-level at finest). Every dollar figure here is a U.S. local-advertising benchmark used as a directional proxy; growth direction is reliable, exact Reno levels are not. Category cards

The "gaming" parallel — what to dive into this year

Reading across the seven, the strongest "dive-deeper" candidates
  • ⭐ Casino & gaming-resort marketing (under Travel & Tourism) — the most defensible Reno-specific thesis. Casino tourism is a $61.4B→$103.9B market (5.4% CAGR to 2034), travel is the fastest-growing ad industry nationally, and Reno's economy is built on it (49% of visitors stay in casino hotels; Peppermill, Grand Sierra, Atlantis, Nugget, Caesars/Eldorado-Silver Legacy-Circus Circus are all major local advertisers). This is last year's "gaming" insight, now backed by category growth data. VERIFY: confirm current Reno casino ad-buying behavior and whether they buy local CTV vs. national/house media.
  • ⭐ Legal — personal-injury & mass-tort CTV — legal CTV ad spend grew +241% in 2025 and CTV now reportedly out-performs broadcast 4.5× for firms. High budgets per advertiser, and a category that has barely started its streaming migration (CTV still ~3% of legal impressions) — i.e., lots of runway. A high-growth, possibly under-served Reno segment.
  • ⭐ Healthcare — Medicare Advantage, urgent care & service-line marketing — healthcare digital ad spend overtakes linear TV in 2026 with CTV budgets up ~60%; Reno's aging in-migration + Renown/Saint Mary's/Carson Tahoe rivalry make health-system and Medicare-season campaigns a recurring, high-value local buy.
  • ⭐ Finance — credit unions & insurance agents — financial-services local ad spend is growing faster (+17.8% H1'25) than almost any vertical; "bank near me" searches +60%. Reno's regional banks/CUs and insurance offices are a growing, under-courted local segment.
  • Analyst inference: the pattern that made "gaming" compelling — a category that is both growing fast and migrating to streaming where Spectrum Reach has a structural edge — points hardest at casino/gaming, legal CTV, and healthcare CTV for Reno.

Method, conventions & data gaps

How sizing/growth was derived, and what to confirm before presenting
  • Fact vs. inference. Dollar figures and growth rates are facts from the cited public sources; the priority ranking and Reno-fit judgments are analyst inference (same convention as the Competitive Landscape Analysis & Heatmap).
  • Why national proxies. No public dataset reports ad spend by category for one DMA. Reno-level category spend is proprietary (Borrell, BIA ADVantage, Kantar — paid, county-level at finest). National local-advertising benchmarks are the cleanest public proxy; treat them as directional, not as Reno actuals.
  • VERIFY before the deck: (1) the BIA local-TV vertical figures (legal $1.7B, auto mfr $989M, pharma $882M) are 2025 local-TV spend, not total category spend — keep the qualifier. (2) Several channel-growth stats (legal CTV +241%, healthcare CTV +60%, auto CTV CAGR) come from ad-vendor blogs (Taqtics, Innovid, Demand Local), not primary research — good for direction, cite cautiously. (3) Reno casino/auto/legal/finance advertiser names are from the project's competitive work + employer lists; confirm each is an active local advertiser, not just a large employer.
  • Ties to existing work. This view extends the Competitive Landscape Analysis §5.3 (white space) and the Reno Opportunity Heatmap (which already carries broadband/affluence/Spanish-language layers you can cross with these categories to find where in Reno to sell each one).
